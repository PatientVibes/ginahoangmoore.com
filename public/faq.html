SNAP Benefits (Food Stamps)

Can I get SNAP if I'm working?

Yes. Many working families qualify for SNAP. Eligibility depends on your household income, size, and expenses. Working does not automatically disqualify you.

What do I need to apply for SNAP?

  • Photo ID for all adults
  • Social Security numbers for household members
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, unemployment, etc.)
  • Proof of residence (utility bill, lease)
  • Proof of expenses (rent, childcare, medical)

Missouri: Apply at mydss.mo.gov or call (855) 373-4636

Kansas: Apply at dcf.ks.gov or call (888) 369-4777

Will I lose my SNAP benefits during the federal shutdown?

No. You will NOT lose your benefits. November 2025 benefits are delayed but will be issued. Your EBT card will continue to work for existing balances.

Can I still use my EBT card?

Yes. Your EBT card works normally. Any benefits already loaded will remain available to spend.

How much will I receive in SNAP benefits?

Benefit amounts vary based on household size and income. For 2025:

  • 1 person: Up to $291/month
  • 2 people: Up to $535/month
  • 3 people: Up to $766/month
  • 4 people: Up to $973/month
  • Each additional person: Add $220

Use the eligibility calculator to estimate your benefits.

Food Pantries & Free Meals

Do I need to prove I'm eligible for food pantries?

It depends. Most food pantries do NOT require proof of income. Some may ask basic information (name, ZIP code, household size) for reporting purposes only. No one will be turned away for lack of documentation.

Are these resources really free?

Yes. All resources listed on this site provide free services. You will never be asked to pay for food assistance, SNAP application help, or crisis support.

How often can I visit a food pantry?

Varies by pantry. Most allow visits once per week or once per month. Harvesters network pantries typically allow one visit per household per month. Call ahead to confirm.

What should I bring to a food pantry?

  • Reusable bags or box (recommended)
  • ID (some pantries may ask, but not all require it)
  • Proof of address (utility bill, if requested)

Don't let lack of ID stop you - most pantries will still serve you.

What kind of food do pantries provide?

Most pantries offer a mix of:

  • Non-perishable items (canned goods, pasta, rice)
  • Fresh produce (when available)
  • Refrigerated items (milk, eggs, meat)
  • Bread and baked goods

Selection varies based on donations and availability.

Utility & Housing Assistance

Can I get help with my electric or gas bill?

Yes. Programs available include:

  • LIHEAP: Federal Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program
  • Utility company programs: Evergy CarePackage, Spire DollarHelp
  • Community Action Agencies: Direct bill assistance
  • United Way 211: Emergency assistance referrals

View all utility assistance programs ‚Üí

What if I'm facing eviction?

Get help immediately. Call:

  • United Way 211: Dial 211 for emergency rental assistance
  • Legal Aid: (816) 474-6750 (free legal help for low-income residents)
  • Kansas City Eviction Help: kcmo.gov/housing

Do I qualify for utility assistance?

Generally, you may qualify if:

  • Your household income is at or below 150% of federal poverty level
  • You're at risk of disconnection
  • You've received a shut-off notice
  • You're elderly, disabled, or have young children

Each program has different requirements. Call 211 to find out what you qualify for.

Mental Health & Crisis Support

What is the 988 crisis line?

988 is the national crisis hotline. Call or text 988 to reach a trained counselor for:

  • Mental health crisis
  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Emotional distress
  • Substance use concerns

Available 24/7, confidential, and free.

Can I get free counseling?

Yes. Options include:

  • Swope Health: Sliding scale mental health services
  • Samuel U. Rodgers: Integrated behavioral health
  • Catholic Charities: Counseling and referrals
  • ReDiscover: Mental health crisis services

Is it confidential if I call for help?

Yes. All crisis hotlines and mental health services are confidential. Your personal information is protected by law.
